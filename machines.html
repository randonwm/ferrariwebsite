Ferrari 125 F1

The Ferrari 125 F1 was Ferrari's first-ever Formula 1 car, marking the team's entry into Grand Prix racing in 1948. Designed by Gioachino Colombo, the car featured a 1.5-liter supercharged V12 engine, producing around 230 horsepower—a bold choice that set Ferrari apart from its competitors. The chassis was a tubular steel frame with independent front suspension and a live rear axle, a design that was competitive for its time.

Ferrari F2001

The Ferrari F2001 was the car used by Michael Schumacher and Rubens Barrichello during the 2001 Formula 1 season. Designed by Rory Byrne and Ross Brawn, it featured a 3.0L V10 engine and introduced an innovative aerodynamics package, including a unique high-nose design. The car was dominant, helping Schumacher win his fourth Drivers' Championship and Ferrari secure the Constructors’ title.

Ferrari SF-25

The Ferrari SF-25 is Scuderia Ferrari’s challenger for the 2025 Formula 1 season, designed to bring the team back to championship contention. Featuring a 1.6-liter turbocharged V6 hybrid engine, the SF-25 continues Ferrari’s tradition of innovation, pairing raw power with cutting-edge aerodynamics. The car was developed at Maranello with a focus on improving cornering speed and tire management—key weaknesses of its predecessors.

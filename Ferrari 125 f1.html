Ferrari 125 F1

The beginning of Ferrari's legacy in Formula 1 racing.

Car Overview

The Ferrari 125 F1 was the very first Formula 1 car built by Scuderia Ferrari. Introduced in 1948, it marked the start of Ferrari’s illustrious history in the world of Grand Prix racing.

Powered by a revolutionary 1.5L supercharged V12 engine, the 125 F1 laid the foundation for a racing dynasty that continues to dominate over 70 years later.

Key Specs

  • Engine: 1.5 L Supercharged V12
  • Power: 230–280 hp @ 7000–7500 rpm
  • Transmission: 5-speed manual
  • Top Speed: 260 km/h

Technical Highlights

Chassis

Steel tube frame with independent front suspension

Suspension

Leaf springs and hydraulic shock absorbers

Brakes

Drum brakes (mechanical)

Debut

1948 Italian Grand Prix, Turin
